Our Aims

CRK maintains aims which specifically address the needs of the Kenyan people affected by cancer, as outlined on our website and in acknowledgment of the National Cancer Control Strategy. CRK values responsiveness (see Core Values) and when a sufficiently large and imminent need arises, these aims can adapt and include new aims as the Board of Directors sees fit, such as the COVID-19 crisis. The aims of CRK are listed as follows.

A. Research

Given the present paucity of advanced scientific laboratory infrastructure and the frequent late diagnosis of many cancers, there is a pressing need to inform cancer eradication policy by characterizing the impact and state of cancer in the wider population. Such research will provide social and epidemiological insights for the immediate future. We hope to collaborate with other organizations to share cancer knowledge in the context of the Kenyan experience.

A.1 Public needs research

CRK will systematically record and analyze the cancer-related needs of the Kenyan public. We aim to measure and evaluate the needs of communities with respect to support, access, treatment, and other aspects of cancer management.

A.2 Epidemiology research

CRK will conduct epidemiological research to better understand the risk factors, causes, progression, environmental contributors, and management of cancer to inform and influence policy and outreach efforts. We will collect epidemiological data and leverage those already existing from cancer management facilities around the country. 

A.3 Oncology research

CRK will engage in research on cancer prevention, diagnosis and treatment in Kenya. We aim to support and engage in basic, translational, and clinical cancer research with the hope of discovering new biomarkers for early detection and effective therapeutic development.

B. Public Outreach

Cancer Research Kenya will fully embody public engagement and work with local communities to create long-lasting and fruitful solutions to the problem of cancer in Kenya. We will systematically and consistently engage and support the public in addressing their needs to access efficient cancer management services, including information on cancer healthcare facilities, sharing our research work and nurturing cancer research culture.

We will engage with local residents, both individuals and community groups, and the wider public; patients; academic institutions and research groups; other stakeholders and the Kenyan government through seminars and conferences.

B.1 Cancer awareness

There remain many barriers in access to information on cancer management as well as to existing healthcare resources and facilities in Kenya. In remote regions, there are gaps in knowledge about cancer, risk factors, and prevention strategies. CRK will actively promote cancer awareness and education, for lifestyle changes and behavior modification through online and in-person programs in remote villages. CRK will share information on available cancer management facilities to encourage high-risk populations to go for screening and cancer patients to access timely treatment. The organization will also develop recall and reminder systems to alert physicians and patients with screening information and schedules.

B.2 Community engagement

CRK will endeavor to increase public engagement and trust in Kenya’s cancer health infrastructure by educating the public about resource offerings at health clinics and working to increase access to treatment and screening in remote areas. CRK intends to share its research output with the public to encourage public discourse on science and medical research. 

CRK hopes to foster public participation and engagement by, for example, inviting patients to take part in research and awareness activities, involving them in research open days, and giving tours of our research facilities. Public participation and engagement in CRK will help demystify the process of medical research, highlight its importance to cancer eradication efforts, and build public support for scientific solutions. Patient involvement will also provide invaluable feedback and input to improve the quality of our work as well as tailor our research and organization objectives to meet public needs.

B.3 Access and education

Oncology research remains an underrepresented field in Kenya. Through our outreach activities, we hope to inspire and encourage the next generation of cancer researchers and make young people aware of opportunities for careers in science. Moreover, CRK will offer training placements and internship opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students interested in pursuing oncology-related careers. This will equip students with the skills, knowledge, and practice necessary for their future career goals. 

C. Clinical Infrastructure

Kenya suffers from a shortage of clinical infrastructure in both urban and rural areas, which has impeded access to quality cancer services. Access to standardized care has been further hindered by the undersupply of healthcare workers, facilities, and equipment necessary for modern healthcare such as pharmaceuticals, diagnostics like mammography, and laboratory testing. CRK will support clinical infrastructure by improving professional networking, and urban and rural access to resources and technology.

C.1 Healthcare professional networking

CRK will build a network of researchers, healthcare professionals, and other stakeholders in cancer management. We will leverage our broad membership and network to address the challenges of healthcare worker shortages. To enhance and promote capacity growth, CRK will cultivate partnerships, offer medical training, and implement knowledge transfer programs such as annual symposia.

C.2 Healthcare access in rural areas

CRK will connect individuals and communities to appropriate and accessible healthcare facilities. CRK will support existing facilities to promote their capabilities to the public. CRK will also partner with stakeholders to build new capacity where needed. 

C.3 Healthcare resources and technology

CRK will facilitate the procurement of healthcare resources to equip clinicians in the treatment of cancers. CRK will distribute informational resources or technology donations through partnerships with manufacturers. CRK will distribute acquired resources to areas with an explicitly demonstrated need and longitudinally monitor the impact.

Integrate technology that will lower the barriers to healthcare research and development into the Kenyan healthcare infrastructure. Though costly, such technologies include imaging scanners that enable access at reduced costs.
