Research

Background

Cancer is a highly heterogeneous disease caused by either one or a combination of different genetic changes and molecular events. This heterogeneity introduces a myriad of complexities that vary from person to person even for the same cancer type. Therefore, current research needs to consider these differences to provide a better understanding of this disease and develop effective therapies. In Kenya, cancer is a growing challenge that puts considerable pressure on the public health system. According to data from International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC), Kenya recorded 42,116 new cases of cancer and 27,092 deaths in 2020. These statistics underrepresent the true figures due to the inefficiencies around patient documentation. While the Kenyan government is constantly upgrading the public health system to meet the growing cancer burden, there is limited focus on oncology research both in the academic and clinical settings. This is the gap that Cancer Research Kenya seeks to fill by offering evidence-based solutions to meet the needs of Kenyans affected by cancer.

Research Focus

The Research Division is responsible for curating, setting, and executing the scientific strategy of CRK. It focuses on three main areas with an emphasis on providing effective solutions to the cancermenace.

Public Needs Research

CRK will systematically record and analyze the cancer-related needs of the Kenyan public. We aim to measure and evaluate the needs of communities with respect to support, access, treatment, and other aspects of cancer management.

Epidemiology Research

We will conduct a range of epidemiological studies to map the national cancer landscape, to uncover risk factors (both genetic and environmental), and to understand cancer health management systems in Kenya. These studies will help inform and influence effective policy and decision making, both at the national and county government levels. We will collaborate with cancer registries, public policy institutes, and social research organizations to ensure accurate and effective reporting and allocation of adequate resources to combat cancer.

Oncology Research

Our scientists aim to identify and investigate genetic changes and molecular events to unravel the biology of tumors and use this information to develop effective therapies. Through both basic and clinical research, and in collaboration with academic institutions and teaching hospitals, we hope to translate our research findings to meet the needs of patients and adjust our research priorities based on clinical feedback.
